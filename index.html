  • My Journey to Protecting my Data
    • Click to Show Learning about corporate data exploitation, I couldn't accept companies stealing my information to sell targeted products.

      My privacy journey began in high school when I bought my first domain, Starprograms.dev, and built websites using GitHub Pages to understand DNS and web hosting.

      When Google raised prices and added AI to their platform, I switched to Proton for email and LibreOffice for documents. In 2022, I found a Dell Optiplex for $125 at a computer surplus store, initially for malware testing but later repurposed as an Apache server in 2024.

      That year I also set up Tailscale VPN, which became crucial for networking services together. I integrated CloudFlare Tunnels to securely access my server's TCP ports over HTTPS.

      In 2025, concerned about Mozilla adding AI to Firefox, I moved from Firefox's password manager to self-hosted Vault Warden, setting up a daily cron job to restart it due to crashes.

      I would say, throughout this endeavor, key points to remember would be:
      • Reducing and protecting your digital footprint takes time, patience, and study. It won't be a single one and done project.
      • Cloudflares reverse proxies and tunnels are the greatest software created to get around networks that are not controlled by yourself.
      • everything costs money, especially when it comes to protecting your data.
      Thanks for reading!
